Top Tourist Places in Tawang

Discover beautiful monasteries, valleys, lakes, waterfalls and hidden gems in Arunachal Pradesh.

Chumegyatser Holy Waterfall

Chumi Gyatse means "100 Holy Waterfalls" in Tibetan and Monpa tradition. Water flows down the rocky mountain in countless streams, making it one of the most sacred and beautiful attractions near Tawang.

Bum La Pass

Located about 37 km from Tawang near the India–China border, Bum La Pass stands at approximately 15,200 ft above sea level and is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Arunachal Pradesh.

Sela Pass

One of the highest motorable mountain passes in India, Sela Pass is situated at around 13,700 ft on the route between Dirang and Tawang, offering breathtaking views of snow-covered peaks and alpine lakes.

Mago Village

Mago is one of the most beautiful and remote villages in Arunachal Pradesh. Known for ancient monasteries, yak grazing fields, rich Monpa culture, and untouched Himalayan landscapes.

Gongkhar La

A stunning high-altitude mountain pass near Mago featuring crystal-clear alpine lakes, winter snow scenery, and unexplored natural beauty, making it a paradise for photographers and adventure lovers.

Tawang Monastery

Also known as Ganden Namgyal Lhatse, it is the largest monastery in India and the second-largest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in the world, founded in 1680–1681.

Zemithang (Phangchen)

A peaceful destination near the Indo-China border, Zemithang is famous for scenic valleys, vibrant Monpa culture, Buddhist heritage, and breathtaking Himalayan landscapes.

Dirang Monastery

Also known as Dirang Gompa, this beautiful monastery is located in the picturesque Dirang Valley and serves as an important spiritual stop on the journey to Tawang.

Gorsam Chorten

Situated in Zemithang Valley, this magnificent 93-foot Buddhist stupa is one of the largest in the region and symbolizes Himalayan Buddhist heritage and India–Bhutan friendship.

Dolma Lhakhang

A revered Buddhist temple in Lungla dedicated to Jetsun Dolma (White Tara). It is located on the historic route toward Khen-Dze-Mani and Zemithang.

Chaksam Bridge

Traditional iron-chain suspension bridges in Himalayan Buddhist regions were known as Chaksam. This historic bridge dates back to the 16th century and reflects ancient engineering excellence.
